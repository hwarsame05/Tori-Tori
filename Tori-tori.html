The story

As the piercing screech of a once-common bird of prey becomes increasingly rare in the African skies, it's clear that something is gravely amiss. Raptors, which were once a ubiquitous sight throughout the continent, are now on the brink of extinction. From the majestic African fish eagle to the formidable martial eagle, these iconic birds are disappearing at an alarming rate due to habitat loss, hunting, and other human activities. Join us on a journey to learn about the plight of these magnificent creatures and discover what we can do to save them from the brink of extinction.

Action taken

The clock is ticking for Africa's raptors, and time is running out to save them from extinction. But in the face of this crisis, there is hope. From the grassroots efforts of concerned citizens to the work of dedicated conservation organizations, people are taking action to raise awareness about the plight of these magnificent birds and protect their habitats. Even small, everyday actions like reusing non-biodegradable waste and planting trees can make a significant impact on raptor populations. Join us as we explore the inspiring stories of those who are working tirelessly to ensure that these iconic birds of prey continue to soar through African skies for generations to come.

Secretary Bird

Secretary Bird

The Secretary Bird is a large bird of prey found in the grasslands and savannas of Africa. It is distinctive for its long legs and feather crest that resembles quill pens once used by secretaries. These birds are formidable hunters, using their strong beaks to dispatch prey including snakes, lizards, and small mammals.

African Scopes Owl

African Scopes Owl

The African Scops Owl is a small, nocturnal owl found throughout sub-Saharan Africa. With a distinctive call resembling a bouncing ball, this owl is often heard but seldom seen. It feeds primarily on insects and small vertebrates and is a master of camouflage, blending seamlessly into its surroundings during the daytime hours.

White-backed Vulture

White-backed Vulture

The white-backed vulture (Gyps africanus) is a large, dark bird of prey found in sub-Saharan Africa. These vultures are known for their distinctive white feathers on their backs and necks, as well as their hooked beaks and sharp talons. They play a critical role in maintaining the ecosystem by scavenging and helping to prevent the spread of disease. However, their populations have declined drastically due to habitat loss, poisoning, and other threats.

Martial eagle

Martial eagle

The Martial eagle is a large and powerful bird of prey that is found throughout sub-Saharan Africa. With a wingspan of up to 2.6 meters, it is one of the largest eagles on the continent. Known for its incredible hunting prowess and fearless nature, the Martial eagle is a formidable predator that preys on a variety of animals, including monkeys, hares, and even small antelopes. Unfortunately, like many other raptors in Africa, the Martial eagle is facing significant threats, including habitat loss, hunting, and poisoning.

Egyptian Vulture

Egyptian Vulture

The Egyptian vulture, also known as the pharaoh's chicken, is a small, white bird of prey found in parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa. With its distinctive yellow face and long, slender beak, this scavenger feeds on carrion, as well as small animals and insects. Sadly, this magnificent bird is facing population declines due to habitat loss and hunting.

Bateleur eagle

Bateleur eagle

The Bateleur eagle is a striking bird of prey found in sub-Saharan Africa. With its distinctive black and white plumage, chestnut mantle, and bright red facial skin, it is a highly recognizable species. Bateleur eagles are skilled hunters, feeding on a variety of prey including small mammals, reptiles, and birds. They are also known for their aerial acrobatics, performing impressive displays during courtship and territorial disputes.
